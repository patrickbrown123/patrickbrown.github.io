Data Cleaning and Analysis in SQL

Project Introduction

The purpose of this project was to analyse superstore sales data, to generate insights that could support business decision making. The objectives were to identify key sales trends, understand customer behaviour, evaluate product performance, and provide actionable recommendations.

The dataset included transactional sales data from a retail superstore, covering order details, customer demographics, product information, and regional sales. Key fields included OrderID, OrderDate, ShipDate, CustomerID, CustomerName, Segment, Region, ProductID, Category, SubCategory, ProductName, Sales, Quantity and Profit.

SQL Methodology

  1. Data Importing:
    • Connected to the superstore database and created a structured table to store raw data.
    • Imported raw sales data from a tab delimitted CSV using BULK INSERT.
    • Verified import success and record counts.
  2. Data Cleaning & Preparation:
    • Identified and handled missing values, replacing nulls in OrderID, CustomerID and Sales with defaults.
    • Standardized text fields (e.g., trimmed Category, uppercased SubCategory).
    • Corrected invalid dates (ShipDate < OrderDate) and negative Quantities.
    • Flagged outliers based on extreme values for Sales or Quantity.
  3. Data Transformation:
    • Created customer segmentation logic (High/Medium/Low value) based on Sales.
    • Created columns MinOrderDate (first order date) and OrderRelativeMonth (number of months since first order) to the sales data.
    • Used a Common Table Expression (CTE): MIN(OrderDate) OVER(PARTITION BY CustomerID) to calculate the date of each customer’s first order.
    • Calculated OrderRelativeMonth as the number of months since the first order using DATEDIFF(MONTH, MIN(OrderDate) OVER(PARTITION BY CustomerID), OrderDate).
    • Populated the sales data with these new columns by joining the CTE on to the data.
    • This mirrors the cohort logic later used dynamically in Power BI with DAX.
  4. Exploratory Analysis:
    • Computed monthly and yearly sales and profit trends using SQL window functions.
    • Identified the top 5 products by Sales per Category.
    • Computed counts of unique orders, customers and products.
    • Aggregated Sales and Profit metrics by Region and Category.

This provided a clean dataset view ready for modelling in DAX and visualisation in Power BI, enabling actionable insights into sales performance, customer segmentation, and product profitability.

Data Analysis and Visualization in Power BI

Project Introduction

The purpose of this project was to demonstrate data modelling and analysis using DAX, alongside interactive reporting in Power BI. The objective was to deliver reports that highlight key metrics such as sales performance, profitability, customer behaviour and retention. This project uses the cleaned sales data from the SQL project.

DAX Methodology

  1. Data Modelling & Analysis:
    • Created calculated measures for key business metrics: Total Sales, Total Profit, Profit Margin and Unique Customers.
    • Generated a continuous date table covering the full sales period, with columns for Year, Month, Quarter and Day. This was created to enable time based analysis and the use of DAX time intelligence functions.
    • Connected the date from the date table to OrderDate from the sales data, via a one to many relationship in Model view.
    • Designed time intelligence measures for year over year growth comparisons.
    • Implemented running totals for sales and profit to track cumulative performance over time.
    • Grouped customers by their first purchase period to monitor behaviour over time.
    • Developed a customer retention measure to calculate the proportion of customers returning in subsequent periods.

DAX Measures

To transform and analyse the data in Power BI, the following DAX measures were used:

  1. Sales & Profit Measures 
      Total Sales = SUM(superstoresales_clean[Sales])
Total Profit = SUM(superstoresales_clean[Profit])
Profit Margin = DIVIDE([Total Profit], [Total Sales], 0)
Unique Customers = DISTINCTCOUNT(superstoresales_clean[CustomerID])
  2. Year over Year Growth 
      Total Sales LY = CALCULATE([Total Sales], SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR(DateTable[Date]))
Total Sales YoY % Growth = DIVIDE([Total Sales] – [Total Sales LY], [Total Sales LY], 0)

Total Profit LY = CALCULATE([Total Profit], SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR(DateTable[Date]))
Total Profit YoY % Growth = DIVIDE([Total Profit] - [Total Profit LY], [Total Profit LY], 0)
  3. Running Totals 
      Running Total Sales = TOTALYTD([Total Sales], DateTable[Date])
Running Total Profit = TOTALYTD([Total Profit], DateTable[Date])
  4. Cohort Analysis & Retention 
      MinOrderDate(DAXColumn) = 
CALCULATE(
    MIN(superstoresales_clean[OrderDate]),
    ALLEXCEPT(superstoresales_clean, superstoresales_clean[CustomerID])
)

Cohort = FORMAT(superstoresales_clean[MinOrderDate(DAXColumn)], "YYYY-MM")

CohortRelativeMonth(DAXColumn) = 
DATEDIFF(
    STARTOFMONTH(superstoresales_clean[MinOrderDate(DAXColumn)]),
    STARTOFMONTH(superstoresales_clean[OrderDate]),
    MONTH
)

Retention % = 
DIVIDE(
    DISTINCTCOUNT(superstoresales_clean[CustomerID]),
    CALCULATE(
        DISTINCTCOUNT(superstoresales_clean[CustomerID]), 
        FILTER(
            ALL(superstoresales_clean), 
            superstoresales_clean[Cohort] = MIN(superstoresales_clean[Cohort]) 
	    && superstoresales_clean[CohortRelativeMonth(DAXColumn)] = 0
        )
    )
)

Power BI Methodology

  1. Visualisation and Insights:
    • Built interactive reports with slicers for Year, Region, CustomerSegment (High, Medium or Low value), Segment (Consumer, Corporate or Home Office) and Category.
    • Visualised sales trends, top performing products, customer segments and retention metrics and monitored underperforming areas.
    • Used matrices and cross analysis charts to compare sales and profit by region and category.
    • Designed visual storytelling to support business recommendations for inventory management, marketing focus, and regional strategy.

      2. Results

      1. Total Sales, Total Profit and Profit Margin (KPI cards)
        • Total Sales reached $2.3M for the reporting period.
        • Profit Margin is 13%, indicating profitability is positive, but with room for efficiency improvements.
        • Profit Margin varies by product and Region, showing where costs may be higher.
      2. Monthly Sales Trend (Line Chart)
        • Individual months vary significantly, with some sharp spikes that likely reflect promotional campaigns or bulk orders.
        • Slower months (often mid year) highlight potential opportunities for targeted marketing to increase revenue flow.
      3. Running Total Sales (Line Chart)
        • Sales demonstrate a steady cumulative increase across the reporting period.
        • The slope of the curve is steeper in Q4, indicating stronger end of year demand.
        • Growth is consistent year over year, with no major drop offs, suggesting stable revenue momentum.
      4. Category & SubCategory Profitability (Bar Chart)
        • Technology products deliver the highest Profit Margin (17%), making them the most profitable category overall.
        • Within Furniture, Tables stand out with negative margins, dragging down the category’s performance despite strong revenue volumes.
        • Office Supplies shows a mixed picture: some sub categories (e.g., Binders, Paper) are profitable, while others (e.g., Supplies) operate at very low margins.
      5. Sales by Region (Bar Chart)
        • The West region consistently outperforms others, contributing 32% of Total Sales.
        • The Southern region shows the weakest performance, accounting for under 20% of Total Sales.
        • Regional variations suggest opportunities for targeted marketing or operational focus in underperforming regions.
      6. Top 5 Products by Sales (Bar Chart)
        • Revenue is highly concentrated, with the top 5 products contributing nearly 30% of Total Sales.
        • The highest selling product (Canon image CLASS 2200 Advanced Copier) outperforms the fifth ranked product (GBC DocuBind TL300 Electric Binding) by more than 3, highlighting reliance on a small product set.
        • This concentration indicates opportunity to diversify sales across more products to reduce dependency risk.
      7. Sales by CustomerSegment (Bar Chart)
        • Only 15% of customers are classified as “High Value”, but they contribute 40% of Total Sales.
        • 44% of customers are classified as “Low Value”, offering upsell opportunities.
      8. Sales by Segment (Bar Chart)
        • The Consumer segment consistently generates the largest share of Total Sales, accounting for over half of total revenue.
        • The Home Office segment contributes the least share of Total Sales and should be targeted with tailored strategies.
      9. Cohort Retention % (Matrix)
        • Early months after the first purchase show the steepest drop, highlighting the challenge of converting new customers into repeat buyers.
        • Some of the more recent cohorts maintain higher engagement, suggesting the value of strong initial acquisition campaigns.
        • Focus on improving Month 1–3 retention, through targeted follow ups and promotions, which could substantially increase long term customer value.

      Conclusion

      The Power BI reports revealed that Total Sales reached $2.3M, with a 13% Profit Margin, across the reporting period, showing steady growth and strong Q4 performance. Profitability is driven by Technology, but reliance on a few products, weaker regions, and low early customer retention present risks. To support sustainable growth, the business should focus on targeting underperforming regions and segments and strengthening early customer retention. This project demonstrates my ability to combine SQL, DAX, and Power BI to deliver a complete BI solution.
