Business Intelligence Workflow Using SQL, DAX and Power BI

Project Overview

This project demonstrates a complete Business Intelligence workflow. It starts with cleaning transactional superstore sales data in SQL, followed by analysis and interactive reporting in Power BI. Objectives include data integrity, understanding customer behaviour, identifying top products and segments and creating actionable business insights.

Data Cleaning & Transformation (SQL)

  1. Data Importing: Created a superstore database and imported CSV sales data, verified row/column counts.
  2. Data Cleaning: Handled missing values, standardised text fields, corrected invalid dates and negative quantities, flagged outliers.
  3. Data Transformation: Added columns to calculate customer first order date, the number of months since first order and created High/Medium/Low Value customer segments.

    4. Creating Calculated Columns (SQL Snippet):

    
-- Create First Order Date and Relative Month Columns
ALTER TABLE superstoresales ADD MinOrderDate DATE;
ALTER TABLE superstoresales ADD OrderRelativeMonth INT;

WITH OrderRelativeCTE AS (
  SELECT
      CustomerID,
      OrderDate,
      MIN(OrderDate) OVER(PARTITION BY CustomerID) AS MinOrderDate,
      DATEDIFF(MONTH, MIN(OrderDate) OVER(PARTITION BY CustomerID), OrderDate) AS OrderRelativeMonth
  FROM SuperstoreSales
)
UPDATE s
SET s.MinOrderDate = c.MinOrderDate, s.OrderRelativeMonth = c.OrderRelativeMonth
FROM superstoresales s 
JOIN OrderRelativeCTE c
ON s.CustomerID = c.CustomerID AND s.OrderDate = c.OrderDate;

With the dataset now cleaned and transformed in SQL, the next stage was analysis and interactive reporting in Power BI.

Data Analysis & Reporting (Power BI)

Key Results

  • Total Sales & Profit Margin: $2.3M in Total Sales, 12.5% Profit Margin. Strong overall performance, with Q4 driving the highest growth.
  • Category & Subcategory: Technology is the top Category. Within Furniture, Tables show negative Profit Margin — an area for operational improvement.
  • Regional Performance: West Region contributes 32% of Total Sales. South Region contributes under 20%, suggesting a need for targeted strategies.
  • Product Insights: The top selling Product outperforms the 5th ranked Product by more than 3, showing an overreliance risk.
  • Customer Segments: High Value customers (15% of base) drive 40% of Total Sales. Medium and Low Value customers show growth potential — opportunities to upsell and increase revenue.
  • Segment Analysis: Consumer customers contribute over half of Total Sales. Home Office customers show highest profitability — worth prioritising for targeted campaigns.
  • Customer Retention: Cohort analysis reveals the biggest drop off occurs in the first three months, highlighting onboarding and retention opportunities.

Methodology

The following steps outline the approach taken in DAX and Power BI to generate actionable insights.

  1. Sales & Profit Measures: Key metrics were computed using DAX for Total Sales, Total Profit, Profit Margin and Unique Customers. 
    
Total Sales = SUM(superstoresales_clean[Sales])
Total Profit = SUM(superstoresales_clean[Profit])
Profit Margin = DIVIDE([Total Profit], [Total Sales], 0)
Unique Customers = DISTINCTCOUNT(superstoresales_clean[CustomerID])
  2. Time Intelligence Measures: Created DAX measures to track performance over time. 
    
Total Sales Last Year = CALCULATE([Total Sales], SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR(DateTable[Date]))
Total Sales YoY % Growth = DIVIDE([Total Sales] - [Total Sales Last Year], [Total Sales Last Year], 0)
Total Profit Last Year = CALCULATE([Total Profit], SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR(DateTable[Date]))
Total Profit YoY % Growth = DIVIDE([Total Profit] - [Total Profit Last Year], [Total Profit Last Year], 0)
Cumulative Total Sales (YTD) = TOTALYTD([Total Sales], DateTable[Date])
Cumulative Total Profit (YTD) = TOTALYTD([Total Profit], DateTable[Date])
  3. Retention Measures: Created DAX columns and measures to track customer cohorts over time and Retention. 
    
MinOrderDate (DAXColumn) = CALCULATE(MIN(superstoresales_clean[OrderDate]), ALLEXCEPT(superstoresales_clean, superstoresales_clean[CustomerID]))
Cohort = FORMAT(superstoresales_clean[MinOrderDate (DAXColumn)], "YYYY-MM")
OrderRelativeMonth (DAXColumn) = DATEDIFF(STARTOFMONTH(superstoresales_clean[MinOrderDate (DAXColumn)]), STARTOFMONTH(superstoresales_clean[OrderDate]), MONTH)
Retention = DIVIDE(DISTINCTCOUNT(superstoresales_clean[CustomerID]), 
CALCULATE(DISTINCTCOUNT(superstoresales_clean[CustomerID]), 
FILTER(ALL(superstoresales_clean), superstoresales_clean[Cohort] = MIN(superstoresales_clean[Cohort]) && superstoresales_clean[OrderRelativeMonth] = 0)))
  4. Interactive Reporting: Slicers for Year, Region, Category, Customer Segment and Segment. Visuals highlight trends, top products and actionable insights.

Conclusion

By combining SQL data cleaning with Power BI analysis and reporting, this project demonstrates a complete Business Intelligence workflow. The cleaned data supports accurate metrics and insights, while the reports provide actionable guidance for business strategy, including targeting underperforming regions and segments, optimising product focus and boosting customer retention.
